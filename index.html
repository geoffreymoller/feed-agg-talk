Feed Aggregations

What are they?

Why do we need them?

We wanted to keep track of interesting aggregations and surface them in the Featured Feed, then see how it would affect engagement.

What sort of aggregations?
Actions on files, notes, groups, users or apps
For this iteration, We settled on file views and downloads, and notes viewed.
What's the Selection criteria for an activity feed aggregation?
At least one actor has to be a followee OR the user should be following the object.
There should be at least two actors in a network who have taken the same action on the object
How about a 1-slide lightning round backend tech overview?
  1. Workfeed generates activity stories which are sent to Streamie
  2. Workfeed requests featured feed from choosie
  3. Choosie requests messages from Feedie
  4. Choosie requests activity aggregations from Streamie
  5. Choosie applies the selection and the ordering algorithm on the data from Streamie
  6. Choosie caches the selected aggregated activities with the messages
  7. Choosie sends the selected aggregated activities and messages back to workfeed
  8. Workfeed hydrates all the relationships, aggregates the activities, generates a message, and returns the response over the /api/v1/messages/algo endpoint.
What does the client see?
/api/v1/messages/algo.json
What did we have to do to yam.js?

  6. Add an ActivityItem type
  7. Extend the SubscriptionList to support a presence call
  8. Punch in a couple of noop methods on the new list and items to meet the contract expected by the list/renderer heirarchy
